MBBS Fees in Romania for Indian Students

Romania has emerged as a popular destination for Indian students seeking to pursue their MBBS abroad. The country offers a high-quality medical education at affordable fees, making it an attractive choice for those looking to study medicine outside India. Here, we explore the MBBS fees in Romania, along with other essential information such as living costs, travel, and visa requirements.

Overview of MBBS in Romania

The MBBS course in Romania is recognized by global bodies like the National Medical Commission (NMC) and the World Health Organization (WHO), allowing graduates to practice in various countries, including India. The course duration is typically five years plus one year of internship. Romanian universities use English as the medium of instruction, eliminating language barriers for international students.

Fees Structure for Indian Students

The tuition fees for MBBS in Romania vary slightly among universities but generally range from 6,000 to 7,500 EUR per year. This translates to approximately INR 4,80,000 to 6,00,000 annually, depending on the exchange rate. The total estimated cost for the entire six-year program can range between INR 30 lakhs to 50 lakhs, depending on the university and exchange rates.

Here is a detailed breakdown of typical annual tuition fees in some of the top universities:

  • GR. T. POPA" University Of Medicine and Pharmacy, Lasi: 8,000 EUR per year
  • Victor Babes" University of Medicine and Pharmacy: 8,000 EUR per year
  • “Ovidius” University of Constanta: 6,500 EUR per year
  • University of Medicine and Pharmacy Cluj-Napoca: 8,500 EUR per year

Cost of Living in Romania

The cost of living in Romania is relatively low compared to other European countries. Students can manage their expenses comfortably within INR 12,000 to INR 20,000 per month. This includes costs for accommodation, food, and other living expenses. Here's a breakdown of typical monthly costs:

  • Accommodation: INR 20,000 – INR 40,000 (off-campus) and INR 12,000 – INR 32,000 (on-campus)
  • Food: INR 12,000 – INR 20,000
  • Transportation: INR 2,500 – INR 4,000

Travel and Visa Information

To travel to Romania for studies, Indian students need to obtain a student visa. The visa application process requires documents such as a valid passport, admission letter from a Romanian university, proof of financial resources, and health insurance. It's advisable to apply for the visa well in advance to avoid any last-minute issues.

Traveling from India to Romania typically involves a flight with one or more connections. The distance between India and Romania is about 4,209 km, and flights can take around 10 to 14 hours, depending on the route and layovers.

Eligibility Criteria for MBBS in Romania

To be eligible for MBBS in Romania, students must meet specific criteria. These requirements include having a minimum age of 17 years and a maximum age of 25 years. Students should have completed their 12th standard with core subjects such as Physics, Chemistry, and Biology, achieving a minimum of 50% marks for general category students and 45% for SC/ST/OBC categories. Additionally, Indian students must qualify for the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) to pursue MBBS abroad, including in Romania.

  • Minimum age: 17 years
  • Maximum age: 25 years
  • 12th standard with PCB and English
  • Minimum marks: 50% for general, 45% for SC/ST/OBC
  • Qualification in NEET for Indian students

Admission Process for MBBS in Romania

The admission process for MBBS in Romania involves several steps. Students must apply directly to the selected universities by submitting the required documents, including the 12th-grade mark sheet, NEET scorecard, passport, and other documents. Upon evaluation, the university issues an admission letter if the student meets the eligibility criteria. Following this, students must pay the first-year tuition fees to secure their seat. Lastly, they apply for a student visa by submitting necessary documents to the Romanian embassy.

  • Submit application with required documents to the university
  • Receive admission letter after evaluation
  • Pay the first-year tuition fees
  • Apply for a student visa at the Romanian embassy

Fees and Cost of Living for MBBS in Romania

The tuition fees for MBBS in Romania range from approximately €6,000 to €7,000 per year, making it an affordable option for Indian students. The total cost for the six-year program can vary between ₹30,00,000 to ₹50,00,000, depending on the university and living expenses. The cost of living in Romania is relatively low, with students needing to budget around €200 to €400 per month for accommodation, food, and transportation.

  • Tuition fees: €6,000 to €7,000 per year
  • Total program cost: Approximately ₹30,00,000 to ₹50,00,000
  • Monthly living expenses: €200 to €400

Travel and Visa Requirements for Romania

To study MBBS in Romania, students need to apply for a long-term student visa. The visa application process involves submitting documents such as the admission letter, proof of paid fees, valid passport, medical insurance, financial proof, and a police clearance certificate to the Romanian embassy. The visa processing typically takes 4-6 weeks, so it's advisable to apply well in advance of the program start date.

  • Long-term student visa required
  • Submit documents to the Romanian embassy
  • Visa processing time: 4-6 weeks

Global Recognition and Career Opportunities

Graduates from Romanian medical universities enjoy global recognition due to accreditations from the World Health Organization (WHO), Medical Council of India (MCI), and other prominent bodies. This recognition allows them to pursue careers in countries like the USA, UK, Canada, and Australia after clearing the respective licensing exams such as USMLE, PLAB, and FMGE.

Career Paths After Completing MBBS in Romania

Indian students who complete their MBBS from Romania have several career options:

  • Practicing Medicine in Romania or Europe: After obtaining a medical license, graduates can work in Romania or other European countries, benefiting from the EU's harmonized medical practice guidelines.
  • Clearing Licensing Exams for Global Practice: Graduates can clear exams like USMLE for the USA, PLAB for the UK, or FMGE for India to practice medicine in these countries.
  • Pursuing Specialization (MD/MS): Romanian degrees are widely accepted for postgraduate studies in prestigious institutions globally, allowing students to specialize in their chosen fields.
  • Research and Academia: Graduates interested in research can join leading institutions for further studies and innovation in the medical field.
