MBBS Fees in Philippines for Indian Students

Pursuing an MBBS degree in the Philippines is a popular choice among Indian students due to its affordability, quality education, and global recognition. The Philippines offers a unique blend of cost-effective tuition fees and living expenses, making it an ideal destination for international students seeking a medical degree abroad.

Overview of MBBS in Philippines

The MBBS program in the Philippines is structured as a BS+MD (Doctor of Medicine) course, lasting approximately 5.5 years. This program is well-regarded internationally and aligns with global medical standards. The medium of instruction is English, which is beneficial for students from diverse linguistic backgrounds. Recognition from bodies like the World Health Organization (WHO), National Medical Commission (NMC), and Educational Commission for Foreign Medical Graduates (ECFMG) ensures that graduates can pursue further studies or practice medicine globally.

MBBS Fees Structure

The tuition fees for MBBS in the Philippines vary across universities. Generally, the annual tuition fees range from INR 2.27 lakhs to INR 6 lakhs. However, total course fees, including living expenses, can range from INR 25 lakhs to INR 35 lakhs for the entire duration.

  • Annual tuition fees for some top universities start around INR 2.27 lakhs and can go up to INR 6 lakhs.
  • Total course fees, including living expenses, typically range between INR 25 lakhs to INR 35 lakhs for the entire duration.
  • Specific universities like UV Gullas College of Medicine and Manila Central University offer fees starting from approximately INR 4 lakhs and INR 2.7 lakhs per year, respectively.

Cost of Living and Additional Expenses

Besides tuition fees, students need to consider living expenses in the Philippines. The monthly cost of living is generally between INR 7,000 to INR 10,000, covering food, accommodation, and other necessities.

  • Monthly living expenses, including food and accommodation, range from INR 7,000 to INR 10,000.
  • Additional costs include travel expenses to and from the Philippines, student visa fees, and health insurance.
  • Initial expenses also include one-time payments for visa processing, medical tests, and documentation.

Travel and Visa Requirements

To study in the Philippines, Indian students must obtain a student visa. The process involves submitting necessary documents, including academic transcripts, valid passport, birth certificate, and medical certificates. Travel costs depend on the frequency of visits to India and any additional trips during breaks.

  • Students need to apply for a student visa through the Philippine Embassy in India.
  • Required documents include a valid passport, visa application form, admission letter from the university, and medical certificates.
  • Travel costs vary based on individual travel plans and frequency of visits back home.

Introduction to MBBS in the Philippines

The Philippines is a preferred destination for MBBS due to its affordable tuition fees, English-speaking environment, and high-quality medical education. The MBBS program, often referred to as the Doctor of Medicine (MD), is structured over six years, including a one-year internship. This degree is recognized by global organizations such as the World Health Organization (WHO) and the National Medical Commission (NMC) of India.

Eligibility Criteria for MBBS in the Philippines

To be eligible for MBBS in the Philippines, Indian students must meet specific criteria:

  • Qualify the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET), which is mandatory for practicing medicine in India after completing the degree.
  • Achieve a minimum of 50% aggregate marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB) for unreserved categories; reserved category candidates require a minimum of 40%.
  • Be at least 17 years old by December 31st of the admission year.
  • Complete the 10+2 education with PCB as core subjects.

Additionally, some universities may require the National Medical Admission Test (NMAT) for admission to the Doctor of Medicine program.

Admission Process for MBBS in the Philippines

The admission process for MBBS in the Philippines is straightforward:

  • Research and select a WHO and NMC-approved medical university in the Philippines.
  • Fill out the online application form and submit required documents such as 10th and 12th mark sheets, NEET scorecard, passport, and medical certificates.
  • Receive the admission letter and then apply for a student visa from the Philippine Embassy.
  • Pay tuition fees and book travel arrangements to the Philippines.

Fees Structure for MBBS in the Philippines

The total cost for an MBBS degree in the Philippines typically ranges from ₹15 to ₹22 lakhs for the entire program. This includes tuition fees but may vary depending on the university and personal expenses. Living costs are generally affordable, with monthly expenses ranging from ₹20,000 to ₹30,000.

When considering MBBS fees in the Philippines for Indian students, it's essential to factor in additional expenses such as travel, accommodation, and visa costs.

Cost of Living, Travel, and Visa Requirements

The cost of living in the Philippines is relatively low, making it an attractive option for international students. For travel, students need to secure a student visa from the Philippine Embassy, which requires documentation such as a valid passport, admission letter, and proof of funds.

To ensure a smooth transition, it's advisable to book flights in advance and arrange for accommodation through the university or external agencies.

Why Study MBBS in the Philippines?

Studying MBBS in the Philippines is advantageous for several reasons. Firstly, the educational system is well-regarded globally, with many institutions recognized by the World Health Organization (WHO) and the Medical Council of India (MCI). The medium of instruction is English, which facilitates learning for international students. Additionally, the cost of living and studying is relatively low compared to other countries, making it an attractive option for those seeking quality education at an affordable price. Lastly, the climate and cultural conditions are favorable for Indian students.

Top Medical Universities in the Philippines for Indian Students

  • UV Gullas College of Medicine: Located in Cebu, this institution is highly regarded for its quality education and is a popular choice among Indian students. It offers a US-based curriculum with clinical exposure and has affiliations with multi-specialty hospitals.
  • Emilio Aguinaldo College School of Medicine: Situated in Manila, this college requires students to complete a pre-med course before entering the medical program. It is recognized by NMC and WHO, offering a strong foundation in medical education.
  • Davao Medical School Foundation (DMSF): Known for its US-based curriculum, DMSF is located in Davao City and is recognized for its quality education. It has associations with several hospitals for clinical practice.
  • University of Perpetual Help: This institution offers a quality MD program with affordable fees and is well-suited for international students.

Fees and Cost of Living

The tuition fees for MBBS in the Philippines vary among universities, generally ranging from Rs. 2.27 lakhs to Rs. 6 lakhs per year. The cost of living is relatively low, with students typically spending around Rs. 7,000 to Rs. 10,000 per month on living expenses.

Travel and Visa Requirements

Traveling to the Philippines requires a valid passport and a student visa. Indian students must apply for a student visa after securing admission to a medical university. The cost of traveling to the Philippines depends on the time of booking and airline, but generally, flights from India can be affordable if booked in advance. Additionally, Indian students must have a valid NEET score to pursue MBBS in the Philippines if they plan to return and practice in India.

Conclusion

For Indian students, studying MBBS in the Philippines offers a viable pathway to achieving their medical career goals abroad. With reputable institutions like UV Gullas and Davao Medical School Foundation, students can benefit from high-quality education at affordable costs.
