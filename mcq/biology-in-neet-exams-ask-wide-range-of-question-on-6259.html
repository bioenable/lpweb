NEET Biology Quiz

Test your knowledge on biology topics commonly asked in NEET exams. This quiz covers various aspects from cell biology to human physiology, ecology, and genetics.

Perfect practice for medical entrance aspirants in the age group of 15-25 years.

1. Which of the following organelles is known as the "powerhouse of the cell"?

A. Golgi Apparatus
B. Mitochondria
C. Endoplasmic Reticulum
D. Lysosome
✓ Correct! Mitochondria are known as the powerhouse of the cell as they produce ATP.
✗ Wrong! The correct answer is Mitochondria, known for producing ATP through cellular respiration.

2. Which of the following is NOT a function of the liver?

A. Detoxification
B. Production of bile
C. Production of insulin
D. Glycogen storage
✓ Correct! Insulin is produced by the beta cells of the pancreas, not the liver.
✗ Wrong! The correct answer is Production of insulin, which is a function of the pancreas, not the liver.

3. Which of the following structures helps in gaseous exchange in plants?

A. Root hairs
B. Phloem
C. Xylem
D. Stomata
✓ Correct! Stomata are pores on the leaf surface that control gas exchange in plants.
✗ Wrong! The correct answer is Stomata, which are specialized structures on plant leaves for gas exchange.

4. Which of the following is the correct sequence of events in the cardiac cycle?

A. Systole of atria → Diastole of ventricles → Systole of ventricles
B. Systole of atria → Systole of ventricles → Diastole of atria and ventricles
C. Diastole of ventricles → Systole of atria → Systole of ventricles
D. Systole of ventricles → Diastole of atria → Systole of atria
✓ Correct! The cardiac cycle follows this specific sequence of events.
✗ Wrong! The correct sequence is: Systole of atria → Systole of ventricles → Diastole of atria and ventricles.

5. DNA replication follows which mechanism?

A. Semi-conservative
B. Conservative
C. Dispersive
D. Parallel
✓ Correct! DNA replication follows the semi-conservative mechanism as demonstrated by Meselson and Stahl.
✗ Wrong! The correct answer is Semi-conservative, where each new DNA molecule contains one original strand and one newly synthesized strand.

6. Which of the following is a water-soluble vitamin?

A. Vitamin A
B. Vitamin D
C. Vitamin C
D. Vitamin K
✓ Correct! Vitamin C (ascorbic acid) is water-soluble and not stored in the body.
✗ Wrong! The correct answer is Vitamin C, which is water-soluble, while vitamins A, D, and K are fat-soluble.

7. Which of the following hormones is produced by the thyroid gland?

A. Insulin
B. Thyroxine
C. Adrenaline
D. Testosterone
✓ Correct! Thyroxine (T4) is produced by the thyroid gland and regulates metabolism.
✗ Wrong! The correct answer is Thyroxine, a hormone produced by the thyroid gland that regulates metabolic rate.

8. In humans, fertilization normally takes place in the:

A. Uterus
B. Ovary
C. Vagina
D. Fallopian tube
✓ Correct! Fertilization in humans typically occurs in the ampullary-isthmic junction of the fallopian tube.
✗ Wrong! The correct answer is Fallopian tube, where the egg and sperm typically meet for fertilization.

9. The process by which plants lose water in the form of water vapor is called:

A. Photosynthesis
B. Respiration
C. Transpiration
D. Guttation
✓ Correct! Transpiration is the process where plants lose water through their stomata.
✗ Wrong! The correct answer is Transpiration, the process of water loss from plant leaves through stomata.

10. Which of the following is NOT a type of RNA?

A. Deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA)
B. Messenger RNA (mRNA)
C. Transfer RNA (tRNA)
D. Ribosomal RNA (rRNA)
✓ Correct! DNA (Deoxyribonucleic acid) is not a type of RNA.
✗ Wrong! The correct answer is Deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA), which is a different nucleic acid from RNA.
