MBBS Abroad Knowledge Quiz

Test your awareness about pursuing MBBS in foreign countries. This quiz will help Indian students understand their knowledge about opportunities, challenges, and important considerations for medical education abroad.

1. Which country is known for having the most affordable MBBS programs for Indian students?
A) United States
B) Russia
C) United Kingdom
D) Australia
✓ Correct! Russia offers some of the most affordable MBBS programs for Indian students. ✗ Incorrect! The correct answer is Russia, which offers some of the most affordable MBBS programs for Indian students.
2. Which exam is mandatory for Indian students who wish to practice medicine in India after completing MBBS abroad?
A) USMLE
B) PLAB
C) FMGE/NEXT
D) AMC
✓ Correct! FMGE (Foreign Medical Graduate Examination), now being replaced by NEXT, is mandatory for students who complete MBBS abroad and wish to practice in India. ✗ Incorrect! The correct answer is FMGE/NEXT, which is mandatory for students who complete MBBS abroad and wish to practice in India.
3. Which of the following is NOT typically an advantage of studying MBBS abroad?
A) Lower tuition fees in many countries
B) Easier admission process
C) Automatic license to practice in India
D) International exposure
✓ Correct! There is no automatic license to practice in India; graduates must pass the FMGE/NEXT exam. ✗ Incorrect! The correct answer is "Automatic license to practice in India" - this is not an advantage as graduates must pass the FMGE/NEXT exam to practice in India.
4. Which organization in India recognizes foreign medical qualifications?
A) National Medical Commission (NMC)
B) AIIMS
C) ICMR
D) CSIR
✓ Correct! The National Medical Commission (formerly Medical Council of India) is responsible for recognizing foreign medical qualifications in India. ✗ Incorrect! The correct answer is National Medical Commission (NMC), which is responsible for recognizing foreign medical qualifications in India.
5. What is the typical duration of MBBS programs in most foreign countries?
A) 4 years
B) 5-6 years
C) 7 years
D) 8 years
✓ Correct! Most MBBS programs abroad typically last 5-6 years, including the clinical training/internship period. ✗ Incorrect! The correct answer is 5-6 years, which is the typical duration including clinical training/internship period.
6. Which of these countries has medical programs taught primarily in English for international students?
A) Philippines
B) France
C) Italy
D) Germany
✓ Correct! The Philippines offers MBBS programs primarily taught in English, making it popular among international students. ✗ Incorrect! The correct answer is Philippines, which offers MBBS programs primarily taught in English.
7. What is the minimum qualification typically required for Indian students to apply for MBBS abroad?
A) 10th pass with science subjects
B) 12th pass with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
C) Bachelor's degree in any science subject
D) Completion of pre-med courses
✓ Correct! Most countries require a 12th pass with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as the minimum qualification. ✗ Incorrect! The correct answer is 12th pass with Physics, Chemistry, Biology, which is the minimum qualification required in most countries.
8. Which of the following is generally NOT required when applying to medical universities abroad?
A) NEET score
B) Passport
C) 12th marksheet
D) IELTS/TOEFL score
✓ Correct! Many countries don't require NEET scores for admission to their medical programs, though some have started considering it. ✗ Incorrect! The correct answer is NEET score, which many countries don't require for admission to their medical programs.
9. What is the approximate success rate of Indian students in the FMGE exam after completing MBBS abroad?
A) 80-90%
B) 50-60%
C) 30-40%
D) 10-20%
✓ Correct! The FMGE success rate has historically been around 10-20%, which is quite low. ✗ Incorrect! The correct answer is 10-20%, which is the historical success rate for the FMGE exam.

B) Climate of the country
C) Quality of education and facilities
D) Total cost including living expenses
