Congress Leader Kanhaiya Kumar Calls for Regulation of Private Coaching Institutes

In recent days, Congress leader Kanhaiya Kumar has been vocal about the need to regulate private coaching centers in India, particularly those preparing students for competitive exams like UPSC, NEET, and JEE. Kumar's call for regulation comes amid rising concerns over student suicides and the exploitation of students by these coaching centers.

Background and Concerns

Kanhaiya Kumar highlighted the systemic failures in public institutions, which he believes have led to the proliferation of private coaching centers. These centers often charge high fees and make false promises to students and their parents, creating undue pressure and stress on young aspirants. Kumar noted that despite the limited number of vacancies for certain positions, thousands of students are driven to seek help from these coaching centers, leading to a culture of unfair competition[1][2].

Allegations of Exploitation

The Congress leader alleged that many coaching centers exploit students by charging hefty fees and then disappearing after collecting payments. He emphasized that the government should take responsibility for these systemic failures and ensure that vacancies are filled through a fair and transparent process[1][3].

Rising Student Suicides

Kumar also pointed to the alarming rate of student suicides, claiming that about 28 students take their lives daily due to the immense pressure they face. He compared this crisis to farmer suicides, attributing both to systemic failures that create unbearable stress on individuals[1][4].

Proposed Solutions

To address these issues, Kumar suggested several measures:

  • Regulation of Coaching Centers: He advocated for stricter regulations on private coaching centers to prevent exploitation and ensure transparency in their operations[2][3].
  • Strengthening Public Institutions: Kumar emphasized the need to improve the condition of government institutions so that they can provide quality education and reduce reliance on private coaching[1][4].
  • Fair Employment Opportunities: He called for a transparent process in filling vacancies and generating employment opportunities to alleviate the pressure on students[2][4].

New Guidelines for Coaching Centers

Separately, the Central Government has proposed new guidelines for coaching centers, which include restrictions on enrolling students under 16 years old and prohibiting misleading promises about exam results[5]. These guidelines aim to provide a legal framework for managing the unregulated growth of private coaching centers.

In summary, Kanhaiya Kumar's demand for regulation of private coaching centers reflects broader concerns about the impact of these institutions on students and the need for systemic reforms to address issues of exploitation and stress in the education sector.
